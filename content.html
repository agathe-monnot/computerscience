What is Internet?

The internet is a network of lots of computers connected to each other so people can keep in touch. You can use internet on computers, tablet, game consoles and phone (but also on watches, glasses, cameras and cars).

Some computers are connected with cables that run all around the world, underground and under the see. Some computers are connected without any cables at all and it’s called wireless.

Internet is a big part of our life because we can use it to do lots of things like doing our shopping from home, playing games with friends who are far away or sharing funny videos of cats.

Listening to music

People can make their own music and put it on the internet for others to listen to. You can listen to many different kinds of music from around the world!

Watching movies and videos

You can watch TV programmes and films on the internet.
Remember to ask a grown-up to help you do this, and tell them if you see anything that worries or upsets you.

Playing videogames

You can play a game by yourself or with someone anywhere in the world!
You should ask an adult you trust to check it is okay to play with others on the internet.

Calling your friends

You can use the camera on a computer, or on a tablet, or on a smartphone to talk to someone.
The person you are talking to can see you and you can see them.
You should never talk to strangers on the internet.

Sending emails

You can use an email to send a message to someone. You can add a message to a website.
Always ask a grown-up to help you send a message.

Searching for information

A search engine can help you find information on the web.
Type some words in the search box to tell the search engine what you need to know.

How to stay safe online?

Remember the 5 SMART rules when using the internet:

Safe: Keep safe by being careful not to give out personal information (such as your full name, email address, phone number, home address, photo or school name) to people you are chatting with online.

Meet: Meeting someone you have only been in touch with online ca be dangerous. Only do so with your parents' or carrers' permission and even then only when then only when they can be present.

Accepting: Accepting emails, messages or opening files, pictures or texts from people you don't know or trust can lead to problems (they may contain viruses or nasty messages!)

Reliable: Information you find on the internet may not be true, or someone may be lying about who they are. Make sure you check information before you believe it!

Tell: Tell your parents, carrer or trusted adult if someone or something makes you feel uncomfortable or worried, or if you or someone you know is bullied online.

What is coding?

On a computer you can do all sort of things: play games, do your homeworks, or talk to friends. All these things are done using computer programs. But what are programs made from?

Computer programs are made of their special language called code. A computer can’t think for itself and you can’t talk to it like it’s a person. It needs computer program so it knows what to do. Code is made out of words and numbers. Sometimes it is even made from words you already know!

You can learn how to put these words in order so the computer understands what to do. You can chose the words and number to chose what happens in your own program! There are lots of things you can do and it’s your code so you can chose. It’s up to you, and your imagination !
